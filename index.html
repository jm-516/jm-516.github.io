Bio

I have a background in English and creative writing, and I currently work as a library assistant at a pharmaceutical company — where I help employees navigate controlled documentation and maintain the library's metadata. That environment has given me a close look at how confusing poorly written documentation can be, which made technical writing a natural fit for something I already wanted to do.

The pieces in this portfolio — a quickstart guide, an SOP, and a tech support guide — are my first real steps toward doing this professionally. I believe good writing, technical or otherwise, always comes down to the reader. My goal is to create documentation that is accurate and actually useful.

Quick Start Guide for Bose Headphones

I own the Bose QuietComfort Ultra headphones and noticed they don't come with a quick start guide. So I made one. This unofficial guide covers everything a new user needs to start listening, including a labeled diagram of the headphone's buttons and a few common troubleshooting scenarios.

SOP for Filing Documents in a GMP Library

Drawing on my experience as a pharmaceutical library assistant, I wrote this fictional SOP for filing documents in a GMP-regulated library. It follows standard SOP formatting and reflects the kind of documentation I work with daily — though names and identifying details have been changed or omitted.

Tier 1 Troubleshooting Guide

This troubleshooting guide walks intermediate Windows 11 users through diagnosing and fixing a common problem: Wi-Fi disconnecting after the computer wakes from sleep. I wrote it for everyday PC users rather than IT professionals, keeping the language accessible without oversimplifying.
